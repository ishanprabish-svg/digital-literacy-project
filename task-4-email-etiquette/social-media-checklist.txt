The 5 Things You Should Do

1.You should use privacy settings in a way: check your social media settings all the time. Use the "mode when you share personal things but keep your professional profile like the one on LinkedIn "public" so people looking to hire you can find you.

2.You should make your profile look professional: share the things you have achieved the projects you have worked on or the volunteer work you have done. Think of your profile as a resume that shows what you are interested in and what you are good at, not a piece of paper.

3.You should check the facts before you share something: before you share a news story or a claim that is going around make sure it is true by checking it with a source. Being good at using the internet means you help stop information from spreading.

4.You should network in a way: use social media to follow people who are important in your industry your professors and people who graduated from your school. If you engage with what they post in a way you might get a mentor or an internship.

5.You should think about the "billboard test": before you post something ask yourself if you would be okay with it being on a billboard on your campus or outside the office of the company you want to work for.

The 5 Things You Should Not Do

1.You should not post things that're against the law or might be questionable: even if it seems like just a fun party photo, pictures of people drinking when they are not supposed to using drugs or doing crazy things can get you in trouble or make it so you do not get a job.

2.You should not fight with people who are being mean online or bully others: try not to get into arguments on the internet that're heated or disrespectful. Once something is posted or screenshotted it is there forever. Getting angry online can make people think you are not very smart about your emotions.

3.You should not share things that could get you in trouble: do not post where you are right now your student ID number or your class schedule. This helps keep you safe and stops people from stealing your identity.

4.You should not complain about your professors or internships on media: if you use the internet to talk about how much you hate your class or your boss it can be a bad sign to people who might hire you in the future because they want you to be able to keep things to yourself and communicate in a professional way.

5.You should not forget about the footprint you leave behind: do not think that just because you delete a post it is gone forever. There are websites that keep posts and people can take screenshots so the things you post online can follow you around; it is better to be careful, from the start than to try to fix things later.
